My name is Preston Kim. I'm currently studying Marketing at the University of California, Riverside's School of Business. I received my high school diploma at Northwood High School, which was just named 2019's most prestigous high school, nationwide. I received a 1400 on my SAT's and achieved an accumulated GPA of 3.7, qualifying me to make the principal's honor roll for 8 total semesters over the course of 4 years. After high school, I moved to Riverside to attend one of the best UC's and expand my knowledge in the world of Business and Marketing.

I've worked outside doing manual labor, as well as behind the desk pushing paper. My first job was working at a steel mill with my father. I was only 13 years old. This job contained a mixture of back-breaking labor and careful, precise organization. Working at a fully functional mill prepared me to be ready for the unexpected and to always stay focused and organized. As I got older, these lessons retained and only increasingly solidified in my mind as I moved through school and jobs that came my way. My first job out of high school was working the grounds at one of the biggest concert venues in Southern California: The Irvine Fivepoint Amphitheatre. This job had me in charge of watching over 12,000 people every other night. Not only did this job teach me multitasking and working on the fly, but also guest service management and personality skills.

Referenced managers would classify me as organized, hardworking, and a delight to be around. I always strive for perfection and never, ever take the easy way out.I've never been sick, I never show up late, I never take vacations, and I don't celebrate any major holidays. I will work tirelessly for anyone who will hire me, and then some. I've never been fired in my life. I've also never quit a job in my life. The only time I leave a job, is to pursue another job, whether that'd be attending one of the best UC's in California, or working full time at one of the biggest concert halls in California. Whatever the task, I will do whatever it takes to complete it, and that is a Preston Kim guarantee.
